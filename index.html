Unusual Hydration Methods

Drinking Water with a Construction Drill

Normally, you’d pour water into a cup and simply drink it — a straightforward and efficient process. However, some individuals don’t use cups at all. Instead, they attempt to hydrate using something wildly unconventional: a construction drill.

To begin this peculiar method, the drill’s head must be replaced with a spherical or rounded metal piece — ideally something that can safely contain liquids. If the drill’s base is open, you’ll need to seal it using metal or any sturdy material to prevent leakage.

Once the cavity is enclosed, pour the water into this improvised container. For extra flair (or perhaps to enhance the flavor), you can activate the drill briefly to mix the water. After that, simply tilt it and drink — carefully.

While highly impractical and mildly dangerous, this method highlights just how creative — or desperate — some hydration solutions can get.
